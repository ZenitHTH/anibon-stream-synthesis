# Pokémon Radical Red Reference

This document provides a comprehensive guide to **Pokémon Radical Red**, a high-difficulty enhancement ROM hack of Pokémon FireRed. Use this reference when synthesizing or timestamping gameplay segments of this game.

---

## 1. Overview
- **Base Game**: Pokémon FireRed (Generation 3)
- **Concept**: A massive difficulty overhaul designed to play like a "souls-like" or competitive-level Pokémon simulator.
- **Content**: Features Pokémon from Generations 1 through 9, including the Gen 9 DLCs (The Teal Mask & The Indigo Disk) up to version 4.1.
- **Core Goal**: Emphasizes strict team planning, synergy, and counter-play rather than brute-force overleveling or healing items.

---

## 2. Difficulty & Game Modes
When starting a save, the player can customize their experience:

- **Normal Mode**: The standard intended experience. Competitive AI, Set mode only, no bag items in boss fights.
- **Easy Mode**: Allows shift style, items in battle, and slightly lower AI difficulty.
- **Hardcore Mode / Restricted Mode**: Extreme difficulty. Bosses have unfair team structures, hazards pre-set, and key setup moves/weather/terrain moves are banned for the player.
- **Minimal Grinding Mode (MGM)**:
  - **No EVs (Effort Values)**: Effort values are completely disabled for both the player and opponents.
  - **Perfect IVs (Individual Values)**: Every Pokémon obtained or caught has maximum IVs (31) in all stats by default.
  - **Purpose**: Eliminates the need for stat-grinding, allowing players to jump straight into team building and battling.

---

## 3. Core Mechanics & Key Items
- **Soft Level Caps**: Pokémon will gain almost zero EXP once they hit the level cap for the next major boss. This prevents brute-force overleveling.
- **Set Mode**: Forced in major battles (cannot switch Pokémon after defeating an opponent's Pokémon).
- **No Bag Items**: Cannot use Potions, Full Restores, or Revives in major boss fights.
- **DexNav**: Allows searching for specific Pokémon on routes, showing their details and making it easier to hunt for hidden abilities and egg moves.
- **Stat Scanner**: A key item used to view IVs, EVs, and movesets of any Pokémon.
- **Poke Vial**: A key item that heals the party fully, refilled by defeating trainers or at Pokémon Centers.
- **Dream Patch**: Introduced in v4.1, toggles between standard and Hidden Abilities.

---

## 4. Built-in Cheat Codes (Entered on NES in Pallet Town)
These codes help eliminate tedious grinding:

- **`Woyaopp`**: Infinite Rare Candies and Pomeg Berries. Speak to the youngster in Viridian City's grass patch after activation.
- **`SO2Toxic`**: Care Packages. Automatically gives items (stones, berries, held items) as the player progresses.
- **`DexAll`**: Shows all available wild Pokémon on routes via DexNav, even if unencountered.
- **`EZCatch`**: 100% catch rate for all Pokéballs.
- **`TeamPreview`**: Shows the opponent's team preview during battles.

- **New Pokemon**: Ogerpon (with masks), Pecharunt, Terapagos (Terastal form only), Munkidori, Okidogi, Fezandipti, Raging Bolt, Gouging Fire, Iron Crown, Iron Boulder, Bloodmoon Ursaluna, Hydrapple, Archaludon, Polchageis, and Sinistcha.
