# Super Sentai & Project R

## Franchise History
Created by Shotaro Ishinomori and Toei Company, the franchise began in 1975 with *Himitsu Sentai Gorenger*. Initially, the teams did not have giant robots. The "Super" prefix was added after Toei collaborated with Marvel in the late 1970s (producing a live-action Spider-Man with a giant mecha named Leopardon). This mecha concept was integrated into *Battle Fever J* (1979) and became a staple for the franchise. It later became the source material for the American *Power Rangers* series.

## 2026 Franchise Update: The End of Super Sentai & Rise of Project R.E.D.
In late 2025/early 2026, Toei officially announced that the *Super Sentai* franchise is entering an indefinite hiatus after concluding its 49th series, *No.1 Sentai Gozyuger*.
- **The Cause**: The decision was primarily driven by financial factors, as production costs for the series, events, and merchandise were no longer being met by the franchise's revenues.
- **Project R.E.D.**: The broadcast slot previously held by *Super Sentai* has been taken over by a new tokusatsu initiative called **Project R.E.D.** ("Records of Extraordinary Dimensions").
- **The New Era**: The first entry in this new franchise is *Super Space Sheriff Gavan Infinity* (Gyaruban Infinity), a revival of the classic Metal Hero featuring a red-chromed suit. This initiative also directly sets the stage for the highly discussed series *Kakuseihunter Omegahorn*.
