# Kamen Rider

## Franchise History
Created by manga artist Shotaro Ishinomori and Toei Company in 1971, *Kamen Rider* launched the "Henshin Boom" in Japan. It revolves around motorcycle-riding heroes who transform to fight evil organizations. The franchise is divided into three Japanese imperial eras:
- **Showa Era (1971–1989):** Focuses on heroes who are cyborgs created by evil factions (like Shocker) turning against their creators.
- **Heisei Era (2000–2019):** Modernized the series with standalone stories, unique mythologies, and no direct continuity to Showa.
- **Reiwa Era (2019–Present):** The current era, continuing standalone narratives with modern technological themes.

## The Thai Market & Distribution (DEX & Flixer)
Thailand is one of the most critical international markets for Kamen Rider. The franchise's distribution is managed through a strategic partnership between **Toei Company** and **DEX (Dream Express)**.
- **Multi-Platform Strategy**: Toei uses Thailand as a testing ground for modernized distribution. Content is spread across YouTube (DEXclub for kids), terrestrial TV channels like **9 MCOT HD** and **ONE31**, and dedicated streaming apps.
- **FLIXER App**: Supported by DEX, FLIXER is the central hub for Tokusatsu streaming in Thailand. It provides near-simultaneous releases of current series (such as *Gotchard*, *Gavv*, and *MY-TH*) alongside high-quality Thai dubbing.
- **Real-World Events**: Toei and DEX frequently host massive fan events in Bangkok, such as the highly successful **"Kamen Rider 50 Years Exhibition"** held at the MBK Center in 2025, solidifying the strong fan culture.
